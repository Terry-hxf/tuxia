Tool Guide

QR Code Decoder Guide and FAQ

Decode QR code images directly in the browser. Upload a screenshot or photo of a QR code to read the embedded text, URL, or message, then copy the result or open the link.

Best for

  • Read QR codes from screenshots, downloaded images, and photos.
  • Check the actual URL before opening a QR code.
  • Copy decoded text for documentation, troubleshooting, or sharing.

How to use it

  1. Upload a clear QR code image.
  2. Let the decoder scan the image and display the embedded content.
  3. Copy the result or open a decoded URL when you trust it.
Privacy note: QR decoding runs locally in the browser. The image and decoded text are not uploaded to a TUXIA server.

Frequently asked questions

Why does a QR code fail to decode?

The image may be blurry, too small, cropped, low contrast, or distorted. Try a clearer screenshot or a larger version.

Can I check a QR URL safely?

The decoder shows the text first so you can inspect it before opening the link.

Does the QR decoder support photos?

Yes, as long as the QR code is clear enough and the image format is supported by the browser.
